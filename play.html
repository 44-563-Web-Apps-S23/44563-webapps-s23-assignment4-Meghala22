LUKE.

[_Off stage._] It's all right, boys.... Jist leave him to me....Git in there, Mister Revenue.

[REVENUE,

_a Northerner in city attire, without hat, clothes dusty, is pushed through doorway_. LUKE, _a lanky, ill-dressed Southerner, following, closes door_. REVENUE'S _hands are tied behind him_.

LUKE.

You must excuse the boys for makin' a demonstration over you, Mister Revenue, but you see they don't come across you fellers very frequent, and they allus gits excited.

REVENUE.

I appreciate that I'm welcome.

LUKE.

'Deed you is, and I'm just agoin' to untie your hands long nuff fer you to take a sociable drink. [_Goes to stranger, feels in all-pockets for weapons._] Reckon yer travellin' peaceable. [_Unties hands._] Won't yer sit down?

REVENUE.

[_Drawing over chair and sitting._] Thank you. [_Rubs wrists to get back circulation._]

LUKE.

[_Going over to cupboard and taking out jug._] Yessa, Mister, the boys ain't seen one o' you fellers fer near two years. Began to think you wus goin' to neglect us. I wus hopin' you might be Jim Dunn. Have a drink?

REVENUE.

[_Starts slightly at mention of_ JIM DUNN.] No, thank you, your make is too strong for me.
